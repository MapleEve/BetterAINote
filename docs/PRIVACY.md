# Privacy

BetterAINote is designed for private self-hosting. Your privacy outcome depends on how you deploy it, which recording sources you connect, and which transcription or title-generation services you configure.

## What BetterAINote Stores

In a typical self-hosted deployment, BetterAINote may store:

- Recording metadata such as title, source, duration, timestamps, and sync state.
- Local audio files when a source provides downloadable audio and local archive is enabled.
- Transcript text, source-side details, speaker review data, and optional word-level timing.
- SQLite databases for app state, recording library data, transcript data, and speaker data.
- Provider connection settings and service credential metadata needed to operate your instance.
- Worker and app logs generated by your own deployment.

## Recordings And Local Files

Recordings can contain sensitive speech, names, locations, and business information. Treat the audio archive and transcript databases as private data.

Recommended handling:

- Store `LOCAL_STORAGE_PATH` on a disk you control.
- Back up audio and SQLite files together if you need complete restores.
- Limit filesystem access to the user or service account running BetterAINote.
- Do not attach raw recordings or full transcripts to public issue reports.

## SQLite Databases

BetterAINote uses local SQLite files. These files are portable and easy to back up, but they may contain private metadata, transcript text, and credential-related records.

Recommended handling:

- Keep database files out of git.
- Encrypt or protect backups according to your own risk level.
- Avoid uploading database files to public bug reports.
- When sharing schema or error details, remove personal recording titles, source identifiers, and credential fields.

## Provider Credentials

Provider credentials for DingTalk, TicNote, Plaud, Feishu Minutes, iflyrec, or future sources should stay inside your self-hosted deployment.

Do not share:

- Cookies, bearer strings, refresh credentials, or source session values.
- Transcription service keys.
- Title-generation service keys.
- Screenshots that expose private sessions or account identifiers.

Use redacted placeholders such as `<redacted>` when documenting a problem.

## Logs And Issue Reports

Logs can accidentally include source names, recording titles, timestamps, account identifiers, endpoint names, and error payloads. Before sharing logs publicly:

- Remove private recording titles and speaker names.
- Remove cookies, bearer strings, service keys, and source-specific session fields.
- Remove local filesystem paths if they reveal private user, project, or server names.
- Prefer minimal excerpts that show the status code, safe field names, and sanitized error shape.

## Third-Party Services

BetterAINote can connect to external recording sources and optional transcription or title-generation services. Those services may process data according to their own policies.

If you want maximum local control:

- Use self-hosted transcription where possible.
- Avoid sending transcripts to external title-generation services unless you accept that provider's terms.
- Review each source account's export and sync permissions before enabling it.

## Public Documentation Boundary

Public docs and examples should only use placeholder credentials and sanitized sample data. Do not include private deployment values, local database files, raw recordings, full transcripts, or unreleased operational plans.
